Syrah/Shiraz

A rich and sometimes meaty wine from the Rhône Valley of France. The most planted wine grape in Australia.

General Info

Syrah, or Shiraz is typically a dry, full-bodied wine with medium tannins and acidity. The primary tasting notes are blueberry, black plum, milk chocolate, tobacco and green peppercorn. Technically Syrah and Shiraz are the same grape. The difference comes from the regions and climates in which it is grown. Vineyards That operate in cooler climates refer to it as Syrah. Shiraz comes from warmer growing climates like Australia. The warmer climate Shiraz tend to be lush and fruit-forward.
