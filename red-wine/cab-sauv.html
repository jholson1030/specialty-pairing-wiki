Cabernet Sauvignon

The world's most popular type of red wine.

General Info

Cabernet Sauvignon is a very popular wine that is grown all over the world in a wide array of climates. The general flavor-profile is typically full bodied with high tannins and good acidity. This is one of the reasons why it can be aged for so long. This grape usually produces wines with blackcurrant and pepper notes, as well as tobacco, chocolate, cedar and oak that will become more pronounced with age.

Popular Cabernet Sauvignon Wines

Some of the most popular Cabernet Sauvignons come from California. Here are a few examples:

  • Smith & Hook 2019

    • A floral aroma of boysenberry, elderberry, and thyme. With a taste of smokey spices and red-fruit.

  • Rodney Strong Sonoma County 2018

    • Rich in dark cherries, plum, and spices. Medium tannins with an oak finish.

  • Louis M. Martini Napa Valley

    • Blackberry on the nose with a hint of pepper. Firm tannins with notes of jam. A good holiday wine.

  • Ancient Peaks Winery Cabernet Sauvignon 2018

    • Big and bold with intense tannins. Notes of stone-fruit and pepper.

Cheese Pairings

When pairing cheese with Cabernet Sauvignon, you want to look for semi-hard aged cheeses. Here are a few examples:

  • Vermont's Cabot Clothbound Cheddar
  • Taleggio
  • Ossau-Iraty
  • Pleasant Ridge Reserve
  • Gouda that has aged longer then 12-18 months.
